# Mission: Sequences and Series

## Why
Pass the quiz on 20–24 July 2026 (ค30107, Week 9A) and the corresponding section of the final exam — with enough real understanding to solve unseen problems, not just repeat worked examples. Sequences and Series account for 10 of the 25 quiz points.

## Success looks like
- Can state whether a given sequence converges or diverges, and find the limit if it converges
- Can apply the Squeezing Theorem to sequences that can't be evaluated directly
- Can identify and sum a geometric series, telescoping series, and recognize the harmonic series
- Can distinguish finite partial sums from infinite series sums
- Can solve PISA-style word problems involving sequences and series (5 pts on quiz)

## Constraints
- Quiz is on 20–24 July 2026 — about 11 days away
- Exam format: fill-in and show-work (not multiple choice in the real exam — practice must build procedural fluency)
- Classroom instruction (Weeks 1–3) already completed; this is review and deepening

## Out of scope
- Convergence tests beyond what's in the course (ratio test, root test, etc.) — not in ค30107
- Power series and Taylor series — not in this unit
- Formal epsilon-delta proofs of sequence limits
