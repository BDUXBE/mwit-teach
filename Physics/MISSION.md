# Mission: Modern Physics — Sci30105 (MWIT)

## Why
To catch up on all five lecture files from the Sci30105 Modern Physics course at Mahidol Wittayanusorn School and pass the exam with a solid understanding — not just memorized formulas, but the ability to apply equations correctly to problems.

## Success looks like
- Can explain why Galilean relativity breaks down and why Einstein's postulates were needed
- Can calculate time dilation and length contraction for a given speed and proper time/length
- Can apply the Lorentz transformation to convert coordinates and times between frames
- Can compute relativistic momentum, kinetic energy, and total energy
- Can use Wien's law and Stefan-Boltzmann law to solve blackbody problems
- Can explain the UV catastrophe and Planck's fix

## Constraints
- Exam preparation is the priority — coverage over depth where time is limited
- Must cover all 5 lecture files: Lec01–04 (Special Relativity) and Lec05 (Quantum Mechanics intro)
- Comfortable with algebra; some basic calculus exposure

## Out of scope
- General relativity (only mentioned briefly in the course outline)
- Full derivations of Maxwell's equations
- Quantum mechanics beyond Lec05 (photoelectric effect, Compton, de Broglie covered in later lectures not yet provided)
