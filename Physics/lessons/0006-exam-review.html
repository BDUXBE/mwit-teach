Part A — Formula Sheet

Memorise these. Verify the constants against your exam sheet.

Lorentz Factor
γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²)
β = v/c
γ = 1/√(1 − β²)
Time Dilation
Δt = γ·Δt₀
(Δt₀ = proper time, shortest)
Length Contraction
L = L₀/γ
(L₀ = proper length, longest)
Only along direction of motion
Lorentz Transform O→O′
x′ = γ(x − vt)
y′ = y, z′ = z
t′ = γ(t − vx/c²)
Lorentz Transform O′→O
x = γ(x′ + vt′)
y = y′, z = z′
t = γ(t′ + vx′/c²)
Relativistic Velocity Add.
u′ = (u − v)/(1 − uv/c²)
u = (u′ + v)/(1 + u′v/c²)
Relativistic Momentum
p = γmu
Relativistic Energy
E = γmc² (total)
E₀ = mc² (rest)
K = (γ−1)mc² (kinetic)
E² = (pc)² + (mc²)²

P = εσAT⁴

λ_max · T = 2.898×10⁻³ m·K

Part B — Common Exam Mistakes

Mistake 1: Using $E = mc^2$ for a moving particle.
Fix: For a moving particle, use $E = \gamma mc^2$ or $E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2$. $E = mc^2$ is only for a particle at rest.
Mistake 2: Confusing proper time and proper length.
Fix: Proper time ($\Delta t_0$) is measured by the clock that is at rest at the event's location. Proper length ($L_0$) is measured in the frame where the object is at rest. Both are the "minimal" distorted values — the clock reads least, the ruler reads most.
Mistake 3: Galilean velocity addition instead of relativistic.
Fix: Any time speeds are a significant fraction of $c$, always use $u' = (u-v)/(1 - uv/c^2)$.
Mistake 4: Wrong sign in Lorentz transformation.
Fix: O→O′ uses minus ($x - vt$, $t - vx/c^2$); O′→O uses plus. Choose based on which frame's coordinates you know.
Mistake 5: Forgetting to convert units (nm↔m, MeV↔J, μs↔s).
Fix: Wien gives λ in metres (must convert if question asks nm). Energy in eV: 1 eV = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ J.

Part C — Mixed Practice Quiz

M1. A ship travels at $v = 0.9c$. Crew ages 5 years (proper time). How long did Earth observers wait?
M2. A proton ($m_pc^2 = 938.3$ MeV) moves with $\gamma = 5$. What is its kinetic energy?
M3. A human body emits most radiation at $\lambda_{max} = 10\;\mu\text{m}$ (10 micrometres). What surface temperature does this imply?
M4. A photon has energy $E = 3.0$ eV. What is its momentum?
M5. What is the single most important feature of Planck's hypothesis that classical physics lacked?

Part D — Recommended Study Order

Read Lesson 1 first — it gives the "why" behind all of special relativity. Without it, the formulas are just symbols.
Do all quizzes in Lesson 2 (time dilation/length contraction) without looking at formulas — this is the highest-frequency exam topic.
Practise Lesson 3 (Lorentz Transform) by covering the equations and reproducing the O→O′ and O′→O tables from memory.
Work Lesson 4 problems. Focus on correctly identifying whether you need $p$, $K$, $E$, or $E_0$.
For Lesson 5, practise plugging numbers into Stefan–Boltzmann and Wien's law. Both appear in calculation-heavy exam questions.
Return to this review lesson and do Part C (mixed quiz) without notes. Any wrong answer = reread that lesson before the exam.
